What is MI?

MI refers to any information used internally in an organisation by managers for decision-making, financial or non-financial. There is no set criteria for collecting, formatting or using management information and the type and quantity of information used will vary by organisation and departments within an organisation.

What is GIST and what does it show?

GIST is the Government Interrogating Spending Tool. It provides a publicly-accessible visualisation of government spending, both according to government financial information (OSCAR) or management information (QDS), all in one place.

What are the main features of the tool?

The tool will allow users to view government spend data using a variety of visualisations through several levels of detail, as well as download the data for themselves.

How often will the data be updated?

The data will be updated quarterly.

Why isn’t doesn’t it update in real time?

Accounting information is always historic; this is to ensure adequate time for the information to be reviewed and any needed adjustments to be made.

What is OSCAR?

OSCAR is a reporting system used by Treasury to carry out its financial management functions (what does this mean) in relation to departmental budgets and estimates for approval by the UK Parliament.

Why are total spend amounts different in OSCAR and QDS?

The scope of OSCAR and QDS are different; the requirement for QDS is (to capture?) 75% of departmental spend. Additionally, many items are covered in OSCAR that are not subject to QDS.

What is DEL?

DEL spend is spend that departments are responsible for, including running and procurement costs, subsidies and grants paid to the private sector, capital depreciation and receipts, and reserves.

What is AME?

AME is made up of Departmental spending programmes (such as social security and public sector), net payments to the European Union, and central government debt interest.
